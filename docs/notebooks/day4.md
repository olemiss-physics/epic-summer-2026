## Science communication

Before poster work begins, we'll practice turning a technical result into a short explanation for a mixed audience.

You'll work on:

- Stating the physics question in one sentence
- Choosing one or two plots that carry the story
- Explaining axes, labels, and uncertainty clearly
- Writing captions that help the reader understand the result

---

## Poster prep

After lunch, use the afternoon project block to finalize your plots and build your team poster for Friday.

**Your poster should include:**

1. What physics question were you investigating?
2. What data did you use and how did you analyze it?
3. Your key plot or plots, with clear labels and captions
4. What does your result mean physically?
5. One thing you'd do differently or explore next

!!! tip "Keep it simple"
    You're presenting to a mixed audience — some know physics, some don't. Aim to explain your result so that a curious non-scientist could follow along. That's a skill professional physicists practice throughout their careers.
