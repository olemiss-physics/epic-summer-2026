## Lodging

On-campus housing is not available through EPIC. Participants and families should arrange and book their own lodging if they need to stay in Oxford.

Participants should plan to arrive by **8:50 AM** each morning. Parking information will be sent with your confirmation email.

## Parking on campus

All vehicles parked on the University of Mississippi campus need a university parking permit or paid metered parking during enforcement hours. Visitor permits are enforced **Monday-Friday, 7:30 AM-5:00 PM**.

For current rules, permit purchase links, and maps, see the official [Ole Miss visitor parking page](https://olemiss.edu/parking/visitors/).

Key details:

- Daily visitor permits are **$5**.
- Visitor permits can be purchased online and printed before arrival.
- Daily online permits can cover up to seven consecutive days; weekends are included in that period at no cost.
- Visitor parking areas include **The Lyceum Circle** and **University Avenue** near the Welcome Center.
- Visitors with current visitor permits may also park in available non-reserved faculty/staff or commuter spaces.
- Metered parking is also available at **$1.75 per hour** and does not require a visitor permit, but the meter must be paid.

!!! note "Large campus events"
    Visitor permits may not be valid for large campus events such as concerts or some home athletic events. Check the Ole Miss parking page if your visit overlaps with a major event.

### Hotel areas and options

Participants, families, visiting teachers, and other guests who need local lodging should book directly with a hotel or rental provider.

Oxford is busiest during university events, so make reservations early and confirm cancellation policies before booking. Useful areas to search:

- **Near the Square:** convenient for restaurants, shops, and walking around downtown Oxford.
- **Near campus:** convenient for program drop-off, pickup, and university visits.
- **West Jackson Avenue / Highway 6 area:** often has more chain-hotel options and easier driving access.

## Stipend and travel support

Your stipend and support are processed differently depending on your institutional affiliation. This section explains how payments work so you know what to expect. You do not need to take action beyond providing the requested paperwork when asked.

### Subsistence support

Participants will receive a **lump sum of $1,200** for the week, based on 25 hours of program participation. This support is intended to cover housing, meals, laundry, and parking. **Receipts are not required** for this lump-sum support.

The stipend payment will be made at the conclusion of the program. If that timing would cause financial distress, please contact us so we can make alternate arrangements.

### If you are a University of Mississippi student

Your stipend is processed through UM Workday as an **Experiential Learning Stipend** using Period Activity Pay. This is treated as W-2 wages with standard tax withholding, the same as other UM student payments.

### If you are not a University of Mississippi student

Your stipend is processed via **E-Form 13: Payment for Services as Contractor**. This is reported on a 1099 as other income, with no tax withholding. You are responsible for reporting this income yourself at tax time.

The University of Mississippi Accounts Payable office maintains the current Form 13 instructions and payment forms:

- [UM Accounts Payable](https://olemiss.edu/procurement/accounts-payable-page/)
- [Instructions for Form 13](https://olemiss.box.com/v/form13-instructions)

### Travel stipend

A travel stipend of up to **$500** is available upon request **in addition to** the $1,200 subsistence support. Mileage is reimbursed at **$0.70 per mile**, up to **600 miles round trip**.

Travel reimbursement may require additional documentation, such as mileage details or receipts, depending on the expense. The program team will tell you what to submit.

## Daily rhythm

Each day runs from **9:00 AM to 4:00 PM**. The morning session ends at **11:30 AM**, followed by a two-hour lunch break from **11:30 AM to 1:30 PM**. Afternoon sessions run from **1:30 PM to 4:00 PM** and include tutorials, project work, poster preparation, and presentations.

Participants will work in **five teams** on **five Belle II projects**. There are **13 participants**, so most teams will have two or three students.
